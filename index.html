More Than Just a Button

By Jordan Sullivan

You just clicked a button. You clicked it almost automatically, without much thought behind it. Perhaps you thought to yourself, "Ooooh, that's a nice button", or "ooh, a button, I like buttons". But did you consider its complexity past its simplicity? Did you consider that this mere button was able to transport you across planes of technology? I couldn't have made that button a few months ago. Think about that for a moment.

Perhaps you took for granted the power the button had.
When using an event listener in Javascript, often the "click" of a button can trigger a whole domino effect of actions in whatever application you are building.

I aim to be a metaphorical version of this "click" as I become a software engineer. I aim to not only showcase my own beautiful complexity, but to also empower those around me who have been taken for granted for too long in this male-dominated technical field. There is no shortage of success and opportunity in the field of technology. This opportunity does exist for women, but a challenge I am finding is the difficulty to recognize powerful female role-models in higher-up positions. If women, and those who identify as women, are meant to climb this ladder, it is more difficult to do so when we can not envision ourselves in these powerful positions.

There is no reason why we can't ALL succeed. I plan to share the message that ANYONE, especially young women can have a successful career in coding if they want to. I plan to spread the message that there IS room at the top for women by climbing to the top myself and making space. I want to be a role-model for young women. I want girls to see themselves in me- someone who learned a set of new skills and practiced her confidence daily. While I am climbing this ladder, I will bring women up with me. I am determined to host free coding courses to guide young women's minds into a future they can clearly see themselves thriving in... an opportunity that was never presesnted to me until my 30s.
As a girl, my mother always gave me the opportunity to voice my opinions, to share my thoughts, and I felt valued and heard. I carried this confidence with me throughout my schooling, and into a few jobs as a young adult. As I became successful, and the time came to move up the ladder at each company I was with, I found myself not being taken seriously as a player in the game. There became no more room for growth. Why could I not climb this ladder? I felt trapped and could see no way to get out of a pattern of poverty I was familiar with. My confidence began to diminish. I found myself constantly asking myself the same question. Could there be a future where one did not have to work 2 jobs, 80 hours per week just to make enough gas money to drive to work again?

When I attended a "Try Coding" course at Turing, I proved to myself that I could do something so new, so out-of-the-ordinary from my beaten-path, that I could begin to see that my future could be shaped in a different way.

That day, I made a button. I made a button from NOTHING, and I felt on top of the world, and it finally all clicked... literally.

I acknowledge the incredible impact that powerful female pioneers such as Joan Clark have had on womens short technological history. My own personal mentors -both women- decided to make a better future for themselves and the world around them by studying at Turing to become developers. These women have been real-life examples of what is possible for themselves, their loved ones, their community, and for their fields. They have never ceased to encourage me to continue striving for a better future for myself while continuing to build theirs.

These women have inspired me to ensure that my own "click" is heard and that my domino effect spreads kindness, empathy, equity, accessibility, and empowers women into more careers, especially leadership roles in tech.
