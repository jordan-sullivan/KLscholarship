More Than Just a Button

By Jordan Sullivan

You just clicked a button. You clicked it almost automatically, without much thought behind it. Perhaps you thought to yourself, "Ooooh, that's a nice button", or "ooh, a button, I like buttons". But did you consider its complexity past its simplicity? Did you consider that this mere button was able to transport you across planes of technology and and transport you to an entirely different technical dimension to this page? Think about that for a moment.

Perhaps you took for granted the power the button had.
With an event listener in Javascript, often the "click" of a button can trigger a whole domino effect of actions in an application.

I aim to be a metaphorical version of this "click" as I become a software engineer. I aim to not only showcase my own beautiful complexity, but to also empower those around me who have been taken for granted for too long in this male-dominated technical field.

I acknowledge the powerful female pioneers such as Joan Clark, and my own personal mentors-both women who decided to make a better future for themselves and the wprld around them by studying at Turing to become developers. These women have been real-life examples of what is possible for themselves, for their comminuty, for their fields, and have never ceased to encourage me to continue striving for a better future. I will follow in their footsteps to ensure that my own "click" is heard and that my domino effect spreads kindness, empathy, accessibility and empowers women into more leadership roles in tech.
